Symptom 1: Migraine Headache

  - Duration    : 4 to 72 hours per episode
  - Description : Unilateral throbbing pain, typically left temporal region.

  - Associated  : Nausea, vomiting, photophobia, phonophobia, neck stiffness

Symptom 2: Visual Aura
  - Severity    : Moderate
  - Frequency   : Precedes 60% of migraine headache episodes
  - Duration    : 20–30 minutes before headache onset
  - Description : Zigzag lines, blind spots (scotoma), flickering lights in

Symptom 3: Nausea
  - Severity    : Moderate to Severe
  - Frequency   : Present in 90% of migraine headache episodes
  - Duration    : Concurrent with headache duration
  - Description : Persistent nausea with occasional vomiting during peak headache.

Symptom 4: Chronic Fatigue
  - Severity    : Moderate
  - Frequency   : Daily (between migraine episodes)
  - Duration    : Persistent, ongoing
  - Description : Patient reports post-migraine exhaustion lasting 24–48 hours
                  (postdrome phase). Poor sleep and dehydration worsen fatigue.

Symptom 5: Neck Stiffness
  - Severity    : Mild to Moderate
  - Frequency   : Present in 70% of migraine headache episodes
  - Duration    : Concurrent with headache, persists 12–24 hours after
  - Description : Stiffness and tenderness in cervical muscles, particularly
                  trapezius and SCM. Correlates with migraine headache onset.

Symptom 6: Photophobia (Light Sensitivity)
  - Severity    : Severe
  - Frequency   : Present in 100% of migraine headache episodes
  - Duration    : Concurrent with headache
  - Description : Extreme sensitivity to light requiring darkened room during
                  migraine episodes. Bright light exposure triggers headache.

  - Type        : Prescription — Beta-blocker (preventive)

  - Indication  : Prophylactic prevention of migraine headache episodes
  - Instructions: Take daily; do not stop abruptly. Review efficacy at 3 months.

Medication 3: Amitriptyline
  - Type        : Prescription — Tricyclic antidepressant (preventive)
  - Dosage      : 25 mg orally at bedtime
  - Indication  : Preventive treatment for chronic migraine headache and
                  improvement of sleep quality
  - Instructions: Increase to 50 mg after 2 weeks if tolerated.

Medication 4: Ibuprofen (Advil / Motrin)
  - Type        : OTC — NSAID (acute relief)
  - Dosage      : 400–600 mg orally with food, every 6–8 hours as needed
  - Indication  : Acute pain relief for mild-to-moderate migraine headache
                  and neck stiffness
  - Instructions: Use for mild attacks only; avoid overuse (max 10 days/month).

Medication 5: Ondansetron (Zofran)
  - Type        : Prescription — Antiemetic
  - Dosage      : 4 mg orally or sublingual at nausea onset during migraine
  - Indication  : Treatment of nausea and vomiting during migraine episodes
  - Instructions: Take with Sumatriptan if nausea is present at attack onset.

Medication 6: Magnesium Glycinate
  - Type        : OTC Supplement
  - Dosage      : 400 mg orally once daily at bedtime
  - Indication  : Migraine headache prevention and improvement of sleep quality
  - Instructions: Consistent daily use required. Allow 2–3 months for full effect.

Medication 7: Melatonin
  - Type        : OTC Supplement
  - Dosage      : 3 mg orally 30 minutes before target sleep time
  - Indication  : Improving sleep quality and reducing sleep deprivation trigger
  - Instructions: Use nightly. Addresses poor sleep trigger for migraine headache.

Medication 8: Caffeine (Excedrin component note)

  - Dosage      : 65 mg (as part of Excedrin Migraine, used on rare occasions)
  - Indication  : Adjuvant analgesic for migraine headache
  - Note        : Use sparingly due to caffeine withdrawal trigger risk.
